# Office of Government Ethics (OGE) Research Brief

**Compiled:** April 12, 2026
**For:** Open Cabinet project internal knowledge base
**Purpose:** Comprehensive reference on OGE structure, authority, disclosure systems, and data landscape

---

## 1. History and Establishment

The Office of Government Ethics was born from Watergate. Congress created it under the Ethics in Government Act of 1978 (Public Law 95-521), the same post-Nixon legislation that established the independent counsel statute and financial disclosure requirements for senior government officials. The agency's mission: prevent conflicts of interest among executive branch employees and resolve those that occur. ([5 CFR Section 2600.101](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/5/2600.101))

For its first decade, OGE operated as a division within the Office of Personnel Management. That changed with the Office of Government Ethics Reauthorization Act of 1988 (Public Law 100-598), which made OGE a fully independent agency effective October 1, 1989. The separation was designed to insulate ethics oversight from political pressure. ([OGE History](https://www.oge.gov/web/oge.nsf/about_our-history))

The Ethics Reform Act of 1989 further expanded OGE's mandate, designating it the "supervising ethics office for all executive branch officers and employees." Executive Orders 12674 (1989) and 12731 (1990) established the foundational principles of ethical conduct for federal employees that OGE administers. ([Wikipedia: OGE](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Office_of_Government_Ethics))

Key legislative milestones since then:
- **2012 STOCK Act** (P.L. 112-105): Required "prompt reporting of financial transactions" for all public filers, creating the 278-T periodic transaction report — the executive branch equivalent of the congressional stock disclosures that Capitol Trades and Quiver Quantitative track. ([Congress.gov: STOCK Act](https://www.congress.gov/bill/112th-congress/senate-bill/2038))
- **2014-2017 Integrity system launch**: OGE built and deployed its electronic filing platform, making all new entrant OGE Form 278e filers submit electronically starting January 1, 2017. ([OGE Integrity](https://oge.gov/web/OGE.nsf/Resources/OGE%E2%80%99s%20Electronic%20Financial%20Disclosure%20System,%20Integrity:%20Off%20to%20a%20Strong%20Start))

## 2. Authority and Limitations

This is the critical thing to understand about OGE: **it is an advisory and oversight body, not an enforcement agency.** It can identify problems. It cannot punish anyone.

### What OGE Can Do

- Review financial disclosure reports for the President, Vice President, and all Senate-confirmed appointees (PAS positions)
- Interpret ethics rules and issue advisory opinions
- Provide guidance to Designated Agency Ethics Officials (DAEOs) across 140+ executive branch agencies
- Order corrective action when conflicts are identified
- Certify (or decline to certify) financial disclosure reports
- Review and approve ethics agreements for nominees
- Conduct ethics program reviews of agencies

### What OGE Cannot Do

- **Enforce compliance.** OGE can order corrective action, but it cannot enforce that order. If an official ignores OGE, the agency has no subpoena power, no ability to levy fines, and no prosecution authority. ([CRS Primer: OGE](https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/IF10634))
- **Investigate.** Criminal ethics violations are investigated by agency Inspectors General and prosecuted by the Department of Justice. OGE refers potential violations; it does not investigate them. ([OGE Enforcement Resources](https://www.oge.gov/web/oge.nsf/ethicsofficials_enforcement-resp))
- **Remove officials.** Even when an official demonstrably violates ethics rules, OGE has no removal authority.

### Who Actually Enforces

The enforcement chain works like this:

1. **Designated Agency Ethics Officials (DAEOs):** Each of the 140+ executive branch agencies has a DAEO who runs the day-to-day ethics program. DAEOs review financial disclosures, identify conflicts, and work with employees to remediate them. Every agency must have at least two ethics officials (DAEO and an Alternate). ([CRS: DAEOs](https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/IF12019))

2. **Agency Inspectors General:** Investigate allegations of ethics violations within their agencies.

3. **Department of Justice:** Prosecutes criminal ethics violations (18 U.S.C. Sections 202-209 cover conflicts of interest, bribery, and related offenses).

4. **Congress:** Can investigate and hold hearings. Senate committees review financial disclosures during the confirmation process.

A 2024 analysis by the Oversight Project described OGE as "a watchdog with a short leash," noting that the director's lack of for-cause removal protection makes the office vulnerable to political pressure. ([Oversight Project](https://oversightproject.org/2024/03/26/a-watchdog-with-a-short-leash-how-the-director-of-the-office-of-government-ethicss-lack-of-protections-creates-oversight-issues/))

## 3. The Disclosure System

### Types of Filers

The executive branch financial disclosure system covers two tiers:

**Public filers (OGE Form 278e):** Approximately 29,000 employees across the executive branch. Required for:
- The President and Vice President
- All Presidential Appointees requiring Senate confirmation (PAS positions)
- Senior Executive Service (SES) members
- Positions classified above GS-15 or paid at 120%+ of the GS-15 minimum ($123,041 in 2024)
- Schedule C employees (confidential or policy-making positions excepted from competitive service)
- Administrative law judges
- DAEOs and their alternates
- Certain Special Government Employees

([5 CFR Part 2634, Subpart B](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-5/chapter-XVI/subchapter-B/part-2634/subpart-B); [GAO-25-107039](https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-25-107039))

**Confidential filers (OGE Form 450):** Approximately 420,000 employees. Lower-level employees in positions with heightened conflict-of-interest risk — contracting officers, procurement officials, grants administrators. These reports are not publicly available. ([OGE 2023 Annual Questionnaire Summary](https://www.oge.gov/web/oge.nsf/publicresources_summary-reports))

### Filing Types

| Form | Purpose | Filing Trigger |
|------|---------|----------------|
| **OGE 278e (Nominee)** | Full financial picture at nomination | Upon announcement of intent to nominate |
| **OGE 278e (New Entrant)** | Entering a covered position | Within 30 days of entering position |
| **OGE 278e (Annual)** | Yearly update | Due May 15 each year |
| **OGE 278-T** | Periodic transaction report (STOCK Act) | Within 30 days of notification, no later than 45 days after transaction |
| **OGE 278e (Termination)** | Departing a covered position | Within 30 days of leaving |
| **Ethics Agreement** | Outlines conflict resolution plan | Negotiated pre-confirmation |
| **Certification of Compliance** | Confirms ethics agreement fulfilled | Within 90 days of confirmation |
| **Certificate of Divestiture** | Documents forced asset sales | Upon completion of required divestiture |

### How Filing Works

Most public filers use **Integrity**, OGE's secure web-based electronic filing system launched January 1, 2015. The system was mandated by Congress, and since January 1, 2017, all new entrant 278e filers must submit electronically through Integrity. ([OGE: 5 Years of Integrity](https://oge.gov/Web/OGE.nsf/Resources/5+Years+of+Integrity:+The+Executive+Branch+Electronic+Financial+Disclosure+Engine))

OGE also maintains Excel and 508-compliant PDF versions for filers who cannot use the web system.

Some agencies run their own supplementary systems. USDA, for example, uses FDonline for agency-level filing.

### The Review Process

1. **Employee files** via Integrity (or paper/PDF for some agencies)
2. **DAEO reviews** for completeness, identifies potential conflicts
3. **Agency ethics officials** work with the filer to resolve any conflicts (recusal, divestiture, blind trust, waiver)
4. **OGE reviews** reports for PAS-level nominees and certain other senior officials
5. **OGE certifies or declines to certify** the report
6. For nominees: **OGE sends the certified report to the Senate** committee handling the confirmation

OGE's performance target: close 98% of reviews within 60 days of receipt. In FY2023, they hit that mark. ([OGE FY2023 Annual Performance Report](https://www.oge.gov/web/OGE.nsf/0/BB5FF70AAC120E0885258ADD006F777A/$FILE/U.S.%20Office%20of%20Government%20Ethics%202023%20APR%20(publication).pdf))

## 4. Public Access

### What's Automatically Public Online

The OGE website hosts financial disclosures for the most senior officials without requiring a request:
- **President and Vice President** — available on oge.gov
- **Executive Level I officials** (Cabinet secretaries) — available via the OGE portal
- **Executive Level II officials** (deputy secretaries, major agency heads) — available via the OGE portal

These are accessible through OGE's [Officials' Individual Disclosures Search Collection](https://www.oge.gov/web/oge.nsf/Officials%20Individual%20Disclosures%20Search%20Collection?OpenForm=).

### The OGE Portal (extapps2.oge.gov)

The Presidential Nominee and Appointee Request System at `extapps2.oge.gov/201/Presiden.nsf` serves as the primary public access point. It lists all nominees and appointees whose reports are held by OGE, with direct PDF download links for Level I and II officials. ([OGE Portal](https://extapps2.oge.gov/201/Presiden.nsf))

### Form 201 Requests

For officials below Level II — SES members, sub-cabinet appointees, Schedule C employees — the public must file an **OGE Form 201** ("Request to Inspect or Receive Copies of Executive Branch Personnel Public Financial Disclosure Reports or Other Covered Records"). ([DOI: OGE Form 201](https://www.doi.gov/ethics/requests-financial-disclosure-reports-oge-form-201))

Key details:
- The request goes to the agency holding the report, not to OGE (unless OGE holds it)
- Reports are available 30 days after the individual files
- Requesters must state they will not use the records for commercial purposes (except news media for public dissemination) or solicitation
- An online version of Form 201 has been available since March 2012
- **Processing time is not formally capped.** In practice, agencies respond within days to weeks, but there is no statutory deadline for processing Form 201 requests

### FOIA

Financial disclosure reports filed under the Ethics in Government Act are generally exempt from FOIA (they have their own access regime under 5 U.S.C. app. Section 105). However, FOIA can be used to request related records that aren't covered by the Form 201 process — agency correspondence about ethics compliance, OGE guidance letters, internal memos about conflict remediation.

### Recent Access Disruptions

The White House financial disclosures page was taken offline following Trump's second inauguration in January 2025. The page had previously offered downloadable 278e reports for the President, Vice President, and senior White House staff. As of early 2025, it remained unclear whether it would be restored. Requests could still be made via email to ethics@who.eop.gov. ([LegiStorm](https://www.legistorm.com/pro_news/3243/white-house-financial-disclosure-page-disappears.html))

## 5. The OGE API

### Endpoint and Structure

OGE maintains an undocumented JSON API at:

```
https://extapps2.oge.gov/201/Presiden.nsf/API.xsp/v2/rest
```

This API powers the public-facing portal and is freely accessible:
- **No authentication required**
- **No observed rate limiting**
- **Pagination:** Uses `start` and `length` query parameters
- **Total records:** 16,858 as of April 2026
- **Data returned per record:** name, title, agency, level (I, II, n/a), filing type, date, PDF link

### What the API Provides

The API indexes every document OGE holds for presidential nominees and appointees:
- **278-T Periodic Transaction Reports** (stock trades — the core data for Open Cabinet)
- **Nominee 278e** (full financial disclosure at nomination)
- **Annual 278e** reports
- **Ethics Agreements**
- **Certifications of Ethics Agreement Compliance**
- **Certificates of Divestiture**
- **Conflict of Interest Waivers**
- **Termination reports**

Key numbers from the current dataset:
- **201 officials** with 278-T transaction reports
- **629 total transaction reports** (spanning July 2025 to April 2026)
- **68 PDFs directly downloadable** (Level I and II officials)
- **561 require Form 201 requests** (officials listed as "n/a" level)

### What the API Does Not Provide

- **Transaction-level data.** The API indexes documents, not individual trades. To get actual stock transactions, you must download and parse the PDFs.
- **Confidential filer data.** The ~420,000 OGE-450 confidential filers are not in this system at all.
- **Sub-Level-II disclosures.** Officials below Level II appear in the index but their PDFs are not directly downloadable — they require Form 201 requests.
- **Historical data.** The API appears to cover the current administration's filings. Prior administrations' records are available through separate archival processes.

### API Limitations

There is no official API documentation. The endpoint was discovered through inspection of the OGE portal's client-side code. It could change or disappear without notice. The data structure has been stable but is not guaranteed.

## 6. Who Files What: The Executive Branch Hierarchy

### Executive Level Breakdown

| Level | Examples | Approx. Positions | Files Public Disclosure? | Available Online? |
|-------|----------|-------------------|-------------------------|-------------------|
| **President / VP** | Trump, Vance | 2 | Yes | Yes (oge.gov) |
| **Level I** | Cabinet Secretaries (Bessent, Bondi, etc.) | ~20 | Yes | Yes (OGE portal) |
| **Level II** | Deputy Secretaries, major agency heads (SSA Commissioner, CIA Director) | ~50-60 | Yes | Yes (OGE portal) |
| **Level III-V** | Assistant Secretaries, General Counsels, bureau heads | ~150-200 | Yes | Form 201 required |
| **SES** | Career senior executives | ~8,000 | Yes | Form 201 required |
| **Schedule C** | Political appointees in confidential/policy roles | ~1,500-4,000 | Yes | Form 201 required |
| **Other covered** | ALJs, DAEOs, employees above GS-15 threshold | ~19,000+ | Yes | Form 201 required |

Total public filers: approximately **29,000** (2023 data). Total confidential filers: approximately **420,000**. Together, nearly 450,000 executive branch employees file some form of financial disclosure. ([OGE 2023 Questionnaire Summary](https://www.oge.gov/web/oge.nsf/publicresources_summary-reports))

### The STOCK Act and 278-T Reports

The STOCK Act's periodic transaction reporting requirement applies to **all public filers** — not just political appointees. Any executive branch employee who files an OGE 278e must also file a 278-T within 30 days of learning about a transaction (and no later than 45 days after the transaction) for any stock, bond, commodity future, or other security transaction exceeding $1,000 by the filer, their spouse, or dependent children. ([DOE: STOCK Act](https://www.energy.gov/gc/stop-trading-congressional-knowledge-stock-act-periodic-transaction-reporting-requirements-oge))

The penalty for late filing: a **$200 fee** per late report (not per transaction). The fee can be waived. Extensions of up to 90 days total can be granted for good cause. ([STOCK Act](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/STOCK_Act))

## 7. The Transition Process

Presidential transitions produce the largest surge of financial disclosure activity in the federal government. The incoming president must fill more than 1,000 Senate-confirmed positions across 140+ agencies. ([Center for Presidential Transition](https://presidentialtransition.org/appointee-resources/ready-to-serve-prospective-appointees/financial-disclosure-and-ethics/))

### Timeline

1. **President announces intent to nominate.** The nominee may begin filling out financial disclosure forms.

2. **Nominee completes OGE Form 278e via Integrity.** They typically have 24-72 hours after receiving a preliminary offer to enter their financial data. The form covers: assets and income, transactions, gifts, reimbursements, travel expenses, liabilities, agreements/arrangements, outside positions, and compensation over $5,000. ([CRS: Nominee Financial Disclosure](https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/IF12736))

3. **Agency DAEO reviews the filing.** Identifies potential conflicts.

4. **OGE reviews and prepares an ethics agreement.** The agreement specifies how the nominee will resolve conflicts — typically through some combination of:
   - **Recusal** from matters involving their financial interests or former employers
   - **Divestiture** of conflicting assets
   - **Resignation** from outside positions
   - **Blind trust** (rare — requires OGE approval and an independent trustee)
   - **Statutory or regulatory waivers**

5. **OGE certifies the 278e and sends it to the Senate.** The relevant Senate committee receives the financial disclosure and ethics agreement.

6. **Senate confirmation hearing.** Senators may ask additional questions about finances.

7. **Confirmation.** The 90-day clock starts for compliance with the ethics agreement.

8. **90-day divestiture/compliance deadline.** The appointee must complete all promised actions (sell stocks, resign from boards, etc.) and submit a Certification of Ethics Agreement Compliance. ([OGE Certification Form](https://extapps2.oge.gov/Web/oge.nsf/OGE%20Forms/5D151C9FCC912A5F85258C7C00591D01/$FILE/Certification%20of%20Ethics%20Agreement%20Compliance-January%202025.pdf?open=))

### Scale of the Problem

The current administration (Trump's second term, beginning January 2025) is processing what observers have called the wealthiest cabinet in American history. The combined estimated net worth of cabinet-level officials exceeds $13.8 billion. This creates an unusually high volume of complex ethics agreements involving large, illiquid assets (hedge funds, private equity, real estate LLCs, cryptocurrency holdings).

## 8. Current OGE Leadership and Recent Controversies

### The Revolving Door: Four Directors in One Year

OGE has experienced unprecedented leadership instability since February 2025. Prior to the current administration, no president had ever attempted to remove a sitting OGE director mid-term. The five-year term was specifically designed to span administrations and insulate the office from political interference. ([CREW Investigation](https://www.citizensforethics.org/reports-investigations/crew-investigations/trumps-unprecedented-meddling-has-turned-oge-into-a-revolving-door/))

|----------|-------|-------|
| **David Huitema** | Dec 16, 2024 - Feb 10, 2025 | Senate-confirmed (50-46 in lame-duck session). Removed by Trump via email from Presidential Personnel Office sent on a Friday night. Had no notice and no explanation. ([CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2025/02/10/politics/office-of-government-ethics-david-huitema-trump); [CBS News](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/trump-office-of-government-ethics-director/)) |

Also simultaneously designated as acting head of the Office of Special Counsel. ([Federal News Network](https://federalnewsnetwork.com/agency-oversight/2025/02/trump-fires-top-government-ethics-whistleblower-officials/)) |

| **Eric Ueland** (acting) | Aug 26, 2025 - present [NEEDS VERIFICATION — may have ended Dec 3, 2025] | OMB Deputy Director for Management. Lawmakers and former OGE director Walter Shaub warned that an OMB official running OGE compromises its independence because OMB controls OGE's budget. ### Why This Matters

Former OGE director Walter Shaub called the sequence of removals and political installations "a systematic effort to erode [OGE's] independence." ([ABC News](https://abcnews.go.com/Politics/former-government-ethics-director-warns-corruption-danger/story?id=118737576))

The practical impact: OGE is responsible for reviewing financial disclosures and certifying ethics agreements during the very period when the administration's wealthy nominees are filing. Having the office led by political allies of the president — rather than career ethics professionals — raises questions about the rigor of that review.

Senator Adam Schiff demanded answers about the removal in a formal letter to the administration. ([Schiff Statement](https://www.schiff.senate.gov/news/press-releases/news-sen-adam-schiff-demands-answers-on-trump-administrations-abrupt-removal-of-director-of-the-u-s-office-of-government-ethics/))

Common Cause described the removal as part of a broader pattern of "weakening government oversight." ([Common Cause](https://www.commoncause.org/resources/trump-weakens-government-oversight/))

## 9. OGE Annual Reports and Compliance Data

### Agency Size

OGE is a tiny agency:
- **Staff:** 74 federal employees (as of September 2024)
- **FY2025 budget:** $22.4 million
- **FY2026 budget request:** $23 million
- **Growth:** Budget has grown from $5.4 million in 1990 to $23 million in 2024

For context, OGE's 74 employees oversee ethics programs supporting more than **5,000 ethics officials** across **140+ federal agencies**. ([USAFacts: OGE](https://usafacts.org/explainers/what-does-the-us-government-do/agency/office-of-government-ethics/))

### Filing Volumes (2023 Data)

- **29,000+ public financial disclosure reports** filed (OGE Form 278e and 278-T combined)
- **420,000+ confidential financial disclosure reports** filed (OGE Form 450)
- **142 agencies** responded to OGE's Annual Agency Ethics Program Questionnaire (100% compliance)
- **98% of OGE's reviews** of public financial disclosure reports closed within 60 days of receipt

([OGE FY2023 Annual Performance Report](https://www.oge.gov/web/OGE.nsf/0/BB5FF70AAC120E0885258ADD006F777A/$FILE/U.S.%20Office%20of%20Government%20Ethics%202023%20APR%20(publication).pdf); [OGE 2023 Questionnaire Summary](https://www.oge.gov/web/OGE.nsf/0/C9CF4DAB1C29884085258B4E00594C5C/$FILE/OGE_2023%20Annual%20Agency%20Ethics%20Program%20Questionnaire%20Summary%20Report.pdf))

### GAO Findings (2024)

A December 2024 GAO report (GAO-25-107039) found that OGE's last comprehensive evaluation of financial disclosure reporting requirements was in **2005** — nearly two decades ago. Congress had not updated the requirements as of October 2024. GAO recommended modernization. ([GAO Report](https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-25-107039))

## 10. Data Gaps: What You Cannot Find

### Behind Form 201

The biggest structural gap in executive branch financial transparency: **only Level I and Level II officials' disclosures are posted online automatically.** Everyone else — and that includes thousands of powerful officials — requires a Form 201 request.

That means:
- **SES members** who make policy decisions affecting specific industries
- **Schedule C appointees** in White House and agency policy roles
- **Sub-cabinet officials** (Assistant Secretaries, General Counsels, bureau heads)
- **Special Government Employees** on advisory committees

All require individual, per-person requests. There is no bulk download. There is no API for these records. You must know the person's name and submit a Form 201 to their specific agency.

### Processing Delays

There is no statutory deadline for agencies to respond to Form 201 requests. While the Ethics in Government Act says reports are available 30 days after filing, agencies have discretion on processing time. In practice, responses come in days to weeks. During transition periods with high filing volumes, delays stretch longer.

### What's Simply Not Disclosed

- **Exact dollar amounts.** All transaction and asset values are reported in ranges ($1,001-$15,000; $15,001-$50,000; etc. up to "Over $50,000,000"). The precise value is never disclosed.
- **Counterparties.** Who bought the stock an official sold, or vice versa.
- **Broker information.** Which financial institution executed the trades.
- **Dependent children's individual transactions.** Lumped with the filer's.
- **Holdings under $1,000.** Below the reporting threshold.
- **Bank accounts, primary residence value, government retirement benefits.** Excluded from reporting.
- **Real-time data.** Even with the STOCK Act, there is a 30-45 day delay from transaction to filing, then additional time for OGE review and posting. Real-time trade surveillance does not exist.

### Confidential Filers

The 420,000+ OGE-450 confidential filers are invisible to the public. Their reports are reviewed internally by DAEOs but never made available outside the agency. This means contracting officers who steer billions in government procurement could hold conflicting financial interests that the public would never see.

### Website and Portal Instability

The OGE website and portal have experienced disruptions:
- The White House financial disclosures page was taken offline in January 2025 after Trump's inauguration
- The OGE portal (extapps2.oge.gov) continued operating but the existing research notes the disclosure page as "frozen since May 2025" [NEEDS VERIFICATION]
- The undocumented API has remained accessible, but with no official documentation or uptime guarantees, it could be altered or shut down without warning

### The ProPublica Benchmark

For comparison: ProPublica's Trump Team Financial Disclosures database covers 1,582 appointees with 3,219 documents. This represents a more complete picture than OGE's public portal because ProPublica filed Form 201 requests at scale. But even ProPublica's data is tables only — no transaction timelines, no compliance tracking, no visualizations.

## Key Takeaways for Open Cabinet

1. **OGE is advisory, not enforcement.** This is not a cop. It is a filing cabinet with an opinion. The real enforcement power lives at DOJ and the agency IGs.

2. **The STOCK Act applies to the executive branch** the same way it applies to Congress. Same 30/45-day filing window, same $200 penalty, same toothless enforcement.

3. **Only ~70-80 officials' disclosures are freely downloadable.** Everyone else requires individual Form 201 requests — which is why congressional trackers have 19+ tools and the executive branch has had zero.

4. **The API is real, undocumented, and fragile.** It works today. It may not tomorrow. Cache aggressively.

5. **OGE has been destabilized.** Four directors in one year, political loyalists running the ethics office, White House disclosure pages taken offline. The institution designed to ensure financial transparency in government is operating under conditions that undermine that mission.

6. **The data gap is the story.** The fact that you cannot easily access executive branch financial disclosures — in an era where 19 tools track Congress — is itself a finding. Open Cabinet fills a structural accountability gap, not just a product gap.
