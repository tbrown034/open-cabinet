# Executive Branch Financial Conflicts of Interest: Research Brief

## A comprehensive survey of news coverage, investigations, and accountability efforts (2025-2026)

---

## 1. Major Investigations and Series

### ProPublica

ProPublica has produced the most sustained investigative coverage of executive branch financial disclosures in 2025-2026. Their key work includes:

**"Explore Financial Disclosures From President Trump and 1,500 of His Appointees"** -- A searchable database at projects.propublica.org covering disclosure records for more than 1,500 Trump appointees, including former lobbyists, industry executives, and officials who declined to identify former clients.

**"More Than a Dozen U.S. Officials Sold Stocks Before Trump's Tariffs Sent the Market Plunging"** (May 22, 2025) -- The marquee investigation documenting well-timed stock sales by executive branch officials and congressional aides before "Liberation Day" tariffs crashed markets on April 2, 2025. Examples include a State Department official selling up to $50,000 in stock two days before the announcement, a White House lawyer selling shares in nine companies before a separate tariff announcement, and a key trade-policy official selling up to $30,000 the week before. ProPublica noted there was no evidence the trades were informed by nonpublic information, but ethics experts said such trading undermines public trust.

**"U.S. AG Pam Bondi Sold More than $1 Million in Trump Media Stock the Day Trump Announced Sweeping Tariffs"** (May 2025) -- Bondi sold between $1M and $5M in Trump Media shares plus $250K-$500K in warrants on April 2, 2025, the same day as the Liberation Day tariff announcement. She had pledged to sell within 90 days of confirmation. Rep. Jamie Raskin demanded an IG probe into the timing.

**"Top DOJ Official Todd Blanche Shut Down Crypto Enforcement While Holding Crypto Assets"** (April 2026) -- Deputy AG Blanche held $159,000-$485,000 in Bitcoin, Ethereum, Solana, and Coinbase stock when he issued a memo ending DOJ crypto enforcement and disbanding a dedicated enforcement team. His Bitcoin holdings rose 34% between the memo and his divestiture. He ultimately transferred his crypto to adult children and a grandchild rather than selling -- a move experts called legal but contrary to the spirit of ethics law. Six senators cited the ProPublica investigation in accusing Blanche of a "glaring" conflict of interest.

**"Documents Reveal a Web of Financial Ties Between Trump Officials and the Industries They Help Regulate"** (March 2026) -- Detailed investigation into Stephen Feinberg (Deputy SecDef) and his ongoing relationship with Cerberus Capital Management, which he founded. At least four companies awarded Golden Dome missile defense contracts are Cerberus-owned. Feinberg filed divestiture paperwork but retained an unusual clause allowing indefinite contracting with Cerberus for tax compliance, accounting, and healthcare.

### NOTUS

NOTUS has built a strong beat covering both congressional and executive branch financial disclosures:

**"The White House's Financial Disclosures for Top Officials Are Incomplete"** (March 2026) -- Reported that the White House failed to transmit financial disclosures for at least five top officials to OGE for second-level review. Border Czar Tom Homan's disclosure had not been updated since May 2025. White House Senate liaison Matthew McMullan's profitable Oklo stock sale in October 2025 was not posted on the White House website until February 2026, after NOTUS inquired.

**"FAA Chief Dumps Millions in Airline Stock Months After His Ethics Agreement Deadline"** (February 2026) -- Detailed coverage of Bryan Bedford's failure to divest Republic Airways stock within his 90-day deadline. Bedford finally sold in February 2026, roughly 150 days late. During the delay, a merger with Mesa Air Group converted his 16,733 private shares into 652,470+ public shares, potentially yielding millions.

**"Rep. Jamie Raskin Opens Investigation Into Howard Lutnick and His Former Firm's Tariff Dealings"** (February 2026) -- Investigation into whether Commerce Secretary Lutnick's family's continued control of Cantor Fitzgerald created conflicts related to tariff decisions.

**"Trump's White House Staff Is Heavily Invested in Tesla, Despite the Elon Musk Fall Out"** (2025) -- About 10% of White House staff holding financial disclosures own Tesla stock. Sixteen staffers disclosed between $800K and $2M in bitcoin holdings collectively.

**Chris Wright New Investments** (January 2026) -- Reported that Energy Secretary Wright made up to $5.25M in new luxury real estate investments while in office.

**CNBC: "Treasury Secretary Bessent says he has divested his soybean farm"** (December 7, 2025) -- Bessent divested 5,000+ acres of North Dakota farmland worth up to $25M, roughly 10 months after his confirmation and 4 months past his initial deadline. Congressional Democrats referred Bisignano for SEC investigation.

## 2. Key Stories by Official

### Scott Bessent (Treasury Secretary)

- **Confirmed:** January 2025
- **Core conflict:** Up to $25 million in North Dakota soybean and corn farmland (5,000+ acres)
- **Ethics deadline:** April 28, 2025 (initial); December 15, 2025 (OGE extended)
- **Compliance failure:** OGE formally notified the Senate Finance Committee in August 2025 that Bessent had failed to comply with his ethics agreement. He said he had completed all but 4% of divestitures.
- **Resolution:** December 15, 2025, six North Dakota recorders' offices received paperwork selling all farmland to Glacial Ridge LLP for over $12 million.
- **Key coverage:** Huffington Post (July 2025), CNBC (December 2025), Banking Dive, South China Morning Post

### Howard Lutnick (Commerce Secretary)

- **Core conflict:** Cantor Fitzgerald, which manages Tether's reserves and holds a ~5% stake (acquired via ~$600M investment in 2024). Tether has been linked to money laundering, sanctions evasion by Russia, Iran, and North Korea.
- **Transfer structure:** Transferred Cantor stake to sons' trust, funded by a loan from Tether itself, secured by Cantor assets including Tether convertible notes. Ethics experts said this could create new conflicts even if technically legal.
- **Family control:** Son Brandon became Cantor chairman; son Kyle named executive vice chairman.
- **USA Rare Earth conflict:** Cantor brokered a fundraising round that USA Rare Earth needed to secure Commerce Department funding. (CNBC, February 2026; Sen. Warren inquiry)
- **Raskin investigation:** House Judiciary Committee opened investigation into Lutnick's tariff dealings and Cantor Fitzgerald connections. (NOTUS, February 2026)
- **Key coverage:** Salon ("5-star rating for conflicts of interest"), NOTUS (Tether loan, Raskin investigation), CNBC (USA Rare Earth), Warren Senate inquiry

### Christopher Wright (Energy Secretary)

- **Background:** Founded Liberty Energy in 2011; held ~2.5M shares worth ~$67M as of January 2025. Made 61 stock transactions (all sales) in Liberty Energy over 5 years. Net worth estimated at $171M.
- **Oklo conflict:** Sits on board of Oklo (small modular reactor developer). Oklo was selected for pilot projects under a Trump DOE program overseen by Wright. Oklo's lobbying spending jumped to $734K in first half of 2025. (OpenSecrets, September 2025)

### Frank Bisignano (SSA Commissioner)

## 3. Historical Context

### Obama Administration (2009-2017)

Obama signed Executive Order 13490 ("Ethics Commitments by Executive Branch Personnel") on day one. It imposed restrictions on participation in matters affecting former employers/clients and barred registered lobbyists from advisory committees. OGE certified neither Obama nor Biden had conflicts. CREW's review found a "strong pattern of compliance" when Obama's approach was later compared to subsequent administrations. This era established the modern baseline for executive branch ethics practices.

### Trump First Term (2017-2021)

Multiple high-profile ethics cases set the template for current concerns:

**Rex Tillerson (Secretary of State, 2017-2018):** Negotiated a $180 million severance with Exxon Mobil to avoid conflicts, on top of $54M in existing stock. Received a certificate of divestiture deferring an estimated $71 million in capital gains taxes. Critics said the government effectively subsidized his transition. After being fired in 2018, he kept the tax break. (CNBC, Fortune, Time)

**Wilbur Ross (Commerce Secretary, 2017-2021):** The most extensively documented ethics violator of Trump's first term. Center for Public Integrity reported Ross failed to divest stocks for months after he was required to do so, and holdings rose in value during the delay. Ross shorted Navigator stock, which OGE's acting director called "an ineffective attempt to remedy your actual or apparent failure to timely divest." The IG confirmed Ross violated ethics rules. CREW filed both criminal and ethics complaints. Campaign Legal Center found violations through a multi-year investigation. (Center for Public Integrity, Washington Post, CNBC, CREW)

**Tom Price (HHS Secretary, 2017):** As a congressman, traded over $300,000 in shares of about 40 health-related companies while sponsoring legislation affecting those companies. The Zimmer Biomet case was especially flagrant: Price bought $1K-$15K in shares, then less than a week later introduced the HIP Act, which would have benefited the company. Price resigned after less than a year over a separate scandal involving private jet travel at taxpayer expense. (PBS, CBS, CNN, Wall Street Journal)

### Biden Administration (2021-2025)

CREW's multi-year review found a "strong pattern of compliance" with divestiture obligations. Cabinet members reinvested divested stock into diversified mutual funds. Some went beyond requirements -- Transportation Secretary Buttigieg voluntarily divested McKinsey-related investment funds. One notable exception: Commerce Secretary Raimondo's spouse retained PathAI stock and exercised ~$65K in options.

Biden signed an executive order on ethics hours after inauguration, described as going "further than any other towards slowing the revolving door." However, Campaign Legal Center found that of 25 campaign ethics promises, Biden fulfilled only 3. His failure to advance structural ethics reform -- particularly a stock trading ban -- was criticized for emboldening the next administration.

### The Contrast

The scale of 2025-2026 ethics issues is historically unprecedented. While first-term Trump had notable cases (Tillerson, Ross, Price), the current administration features: (a) A sitting president actively trading $100M+ in bonds; (b) Multiple cabinet members failing divestiture deadlines simultaneously; (c) The removal of the OGE director and four directors in a single year; (d) Well-timed pre-tariff stock sales by a dozen+ officials; (e) Financial conflicts spanning crypto, defense, banking, aviation, and wellness industries.

## 4. Organizations Doing This Work

### Congressional Stock Trackers

**Capitol Trades** (capitoltrades.com) -- Clean, free interface for tracking congressional stock trades. Covers representatives and senators.

**Quiver Quantitative** (quiverquant.com) -- Aggregates congressional trades alongside lobbying records, government contracts, insider trading, and social sentiment into a single API and dashboard.

**Unusual Whales** (unusualwhales.com/politics) -- Tracks House, Senate, and SCOTUS investing with free access. Publishes an annual Congress Trading Report.

**Others:** InsiderFinance, TraderCongress, Stocknear's POTUS Tracker, SmartInsider's politician tracker, Trendlyne US politician tracker.

**Two ETFs now track congressional trades** (covered by Morningstar), demonstrating the commercial viability of this data.

### Why No Executive Branch Equivalent Exists

The gap persists for structural reasons:

1. **Data access:** Congressional trades are centralized through the Clerk of the House and Secretary of the Senate under the STOCK Act. Executive branch disclosures are scattered across OGE's database, individual agency ethics offices, and the White House website -- with no standardized API for transaction data.

2. **PDF-only transaction reports:** While congressional periodic transaction reports are machine-readable, executive branch 278-T filings are PDFs that require manual parsing. The OGE API returns metadata about filings but not the transaction data itself.

3. **Fewer but larger traders:** Congress has 535 members trading regularly. The executive branch has a smaller number of officials filing periodic transaction reports, but with much larger individual positions (Bisignano's $530M, Trump's $100M+, Lutnick's Cantor stake).

4. **Divestiture expectation:** Executive branch officials are generally expected to divest, reducing ongoing trading. But as 2025-2026 coverage shows, the gap between expectation and reality is enormous.

5. **Commercial incentive mismatch:** Congressional stock trackers have retail investor audiences looking for "trade alongside Congress" signals. Executive branch trades are more about accountability journalism than trading signals.

### ProPublica's Database

ProPublica's "Explore Financial Disclosures From President Trump and 1,500 of His Appointees" (projects.propublica.org/trump-team-financial-disclosures/) is the closest thing to a comprehensive executive branch disclosure database. It covers annual disclosures (OGE Form 278e) and allows search by name, agency, and keyword. It does not parse individual stock trades from 278-T periodic transaction reports into structured, searchable data -- which is precisely the gap Open Cabinet fills.

## 5. The OGE Crisis

A critical contextual factor: the Office of Government Ethics itself has been destabilized.

- **February 2025:** Trump removed OGE Director David Huitema, confirmed just months earlier for a 5-year term designed to overlap administrations.
- **Four directors in one year:** OGE cycled through four people in the top position in 2025 alone.
- **Enforcement capacity:** Trump proposed cutting Inspector General funding by 30% in FY2026 budget.
- **Missing disclosures:** Multiple White House officials' disclosures never transmitted to OGE for second-level review. (NOTUS, March 2026; Sen. Schiff inquiry, December 2025)

This means the watchdog overseeing all of the above conflicts is itself operating in crisis.

## 6. Legislative Response

Multiple competing bills have been introduced in the 119th Congress (2025-2026):

- **Ban Stock Trading for Government Officials Act** (Gillibrand-Hawley, bipartisan): Bans stock trading and ownership for Congress, senior executive branch officials, and families. Increases penalties.
- **Stop Insider Trading Act** (Republican-led): Prohibits new stock purchases by members of Congress, spouses, dependent children. Requires advance notice of sales.
- **No Getting Rich in Congress Act** (bipartisan): Requires qualified blind trusts for all investment assets.
- **Democratic alternative:** Expands ban to include the president and vice president.

Trump called for passage of the Stop Insider Trading Act in February. However, competing proposals have stalled progress. The Democratic expansion to cover the presidency became a dealbreaker for Republican leadership. As of April 2026, no bill has passed. ## 7. Significance for Open Cabinet

This research confirms several things about Open Cabinet's positioning:

1. **The coverage gap is real and documented.** Congress has 19+ trackers. The executive branch has ProPublica's disclosure database (no transaction parsing) and scattered reporting. No one offers structured, searchable, visualized transaction data for the executive branch.

2. **The story is bigger than Congress.** Individual executive branch transactions dwarf congressional trades. Bisignano's $530M Fiserv sale, Trump's $100M+ bond spree, and Lutnick's Cantor transfer involve orders of magnitude more money than typical congressional trades.

3. **Demand exists.** ProPublica, NOTUS, CNBC, Bloomberg, KFF Health News, CREW, and Senate offices are all actively investigating these issues but lack a centralized tool for the public.

4. **The enforcement mechanism is broken.** With OGE in turmoil and disclosures going untransmitted, outside accountability tools become even more critical.

5. **Historical precedent validates the approach.** Tillerson, Ross, and Price cases from the first term demonstrate that executive branch financial conflicts produce major news cycles. The current term has far more officials, far more money, and far less oversight.
